Somewhere along the way, the third-party booking platforms became the middlemen your guests never asked for. They take their cut, they own the relationship, and they flatten every distinctive little hotel into one more search result. We think that's backwards. The more a property leans into what makes it singular, the less it needs to rent its guests from anyone. Distinctiveness isn't a luxury. It's the direct-booking strategy.
